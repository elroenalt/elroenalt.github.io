everything about rockets

To fly to space you will need To build a rocket, though that is easier said than down.
In this mod you cant just build a rocket biger and excpect it to work there is drag and fuel consumtion based on gravity, air drag and mass of rocket and payload.
For most occasions a rocket with 2 engines, eight tanks, a guidance computer, a cargo hold and a seat will be just usable.
To assemble a rocket, you will require launch pad (), structure tower (), place the tower on the side of the launch pad and a rocket assembler () wich will need a power suply.
Using the rocket assembler you can assemble the rocket into a entity by clicking the build button in the assembler.
Fueling the rocket requires rocket fuel and a fueling station (wich will require energy).
To link any ground infrastructure (like fueling stations) to the rocket eather use a linker () and sneak click on the rocket asembler or the rocket and on the target block to link them.
Once you linked the fueling station click the fuel button and the rocket will fuel up, you can also deload a rocket and store it. The fueling station can also be used with pipes from other mods
If you wan to launch to any planet or orbit you will need eather a link or a planetId chip (), the linker can be used by sneak clicking it to landing location and the planet id chip has to be burned in the observatory for satelites there is only distance data required to land you will need composition and mass from the astrobody Data Processor
launching can be done by all of the diffrent launch blocks, currently including launch station, satellite launch station or asteroid launch station (up to now asteroid mining isnt yet added) these blocks will overwrite the landing location in the guidance computer and can be activated by redstone.
satelites

If you want to fly to any planet you will need mass and composition data about it and distance is also required for analysing planets.
To create a satellite use a satellite assembler (), a satellite id chip (), a plane satellite () and all components for the satellite.
All satelites require powersuply for satelites close enough to the sun you can use up to two solar panels () since you will also require a battery ().
Depending on what kind of data you want to collect you will need eather a Optical sensor () for distance, a mass scanner () for mass or a composition scanner () for composition. Also required is a radiation Shield () to protect you from radiation wich has a chance to appear every tick, a LoRa (Long Range) module () to wirelesly transfer data and a data storage () to temporarly store data on the satellite.
After placing the components you can click build to assemble the satellite and burn the satellite id chip to the satellite id. Copying can be down via the copy chip button.
To launch the satellite put the satelite into the cargo hold and use the satellite launch station () linked to the rocket assembler, put a planet chip for the planet the satellite should orbit in and press launch with enought fuel and a suitable rocket. In UI you should see how long the satellite takes until arrivel as soon as the rocket is outside of atmosphere.
It is suggested to launch these out of orbit as soon as possible since this consumes close to no fuel.
To download data us the satellite monitor (), provide it with power, put your satelite id chip in the right slot and press download data.
Taking the data requires a data storage in the top left slot or a wireless transceiver () link to a wireless transceiver placed on consumer input.
building a space station

Building a space staion is crucial for faster travel and more cost effective rocket flight.
First off you will need a space station assembler () wich will need power, a launch pad and structure tower like the rocket assembler.
After building your station on the pad put a linker into the left slot and click build in the space station assembler.
The output will be barrel containing the station and a linker to fly to the space station.
To launch the station put the barrel into cargo hold of your rocket and put the linker into the guidance computer.
Next up sit on the seat (hit the rocket) and launch the fully fueled rocket. This will launch you to the station
To move a station use the warp controllery () put your galaxy database into the slot, select a planet and press travel (the planet has to be analysed).
Using the station controller () you can change your orbit position using the provided sliders (distance, orbitX, orbitY and orbitZ).
The orientation controller () you can rotate the station (yaw, roll and pitch).
Observatory

Using the observatory you can search for planets in the sky.
Searching reuqires an galaxy database () placed in the bottom left slot wich will save all of the data.
To sync databases place the second one in the bottom right side and click sync storage disks.

To search for planets click scan planets wich will search for planets having an random chance to find a body defined in the dimension properties files.
To get the planet data burned to a chip place the planet id chip () into the top slot click "open galaxy", select the wanted planet and click burn to chip.
Needed for burning you will need the required amount of distance data from any planet wich you will need to provide on one of the data inputs and click Analyse the focused planet.
You are also able to see the current progress of collecting the rest of the data (eg. mass and composition) stored on the galaxy database.
Astrobody data porocessor

Using the astrobody data processor you can process data from satelites to fly to planets.
Needed are mass and composition data from sattelites around the planet you want to research in the data hatches () and a galaxy database () placed in the bottom right side to sync the data.
It is suggested to use wireless transceivers to transfer data to the three data input slots.
Placing the galaxy database will transfer the data from the processor to the data.
To fly to the planet you curently need full data for mass, composition and distance.
